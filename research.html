My research guides energy infrastructure planning and technological innovation to assess and advance the role of distributed energy resources in renewable energy systems. I combine techno-economic models to advance transmission planning, resource adequacy contingencies, and physical constraints in cases with increased renewables + storage penetration. My goal is to advance an affordable, reliable, resilient, and renewable grid.

Current Research Areas:

  • Developing energy system optimization models to support policy analysis
  • Informing transmission planning for grid resiliency
  • Generating technoeconomic models to assess resource adequacy

Modeling Energy Reliability and Health Impacts from Solar in the DRC

This paper uses randomized controlled trial data from Congolese hospitals to model how solar power and battery systems improve reliability and reduce blackout-related health impacts.
